Annual Review for 2017

Back to Homepage

At times I wonder how it feels to keep rushing across dreamlands and reality, but in 2017, my second year in IIIS, my life was exactly in this way.

It is sometimes hard to say what I have gained or lost, because they often play like two sides of a coin. I failed to get a prize in the MCM competition, but the experience to learn and work so much within short time made it possible to face following challenges in two semesters. It is sometime difficult to identify what my "wishes" are, but it is clear that most of my decisions are made based on those wishes. Beyond every horizon, probably, lies something I have been always looking for.

However, even though a variety of precious events take place around me now and then, it is destined that only a few of them can be associated with my further way. At that very point, it was high time to move on and start getting onto my own timeline. Having got through all the confusion and hesitation, I did it; and in the future, most importantly, I shall hold on to my initial dreams and strive for the best, as the main theme of 2018.

Beside the decision of future research area, passing GRE and TOEFL at first attempts within a single semester is another relieving achievement. For other parts of life, some minor events also added to the meaning of the year. That aery trip on Line 5 across the skyline completed a long-cherished wish; and the work on some unfamiliar areas, including music recognition and energy allocation, opened up my eyes for a peek of another dimension.

In conclusion, it was a totally fulfilling year in my college life. Yet, I should remind myself: without further hard work in 2018, all preliminary things done in 2017 will be rendered meaningless. Therefore, it was not time to talk about accomplishments --- it was only the beginning of everything.

The year of 2018 is well worth expecting. It is time to keep moving on --- and it always is.
